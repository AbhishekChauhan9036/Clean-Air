Our Clients talk about us

I have been using Airly sensors to study outdoor air quality data and compare them to the indoor measurements. I'm very happy with its easy installation and operation as well as with very quick team support. You can see that Airly Data Platform has been designed with the client and his task in mind, which is why it is very friendly and easy to use.

Joanne Low

Research Associate, Unitec Institute of Technology

There are 40 air quality monitors installed in Opole. Thanks to the Airly Data Platform for monitoring air quality measurements, as the head of the Department of Environmental Protection Planning and Financing in the Department of Environmental Protection and Agriculture of the Opole City Hall, I am able to analyze in which city locations the levels of pollution are exceeded most often. The platform serves us primarily to assess the actions taken - including subsidies granted to change the method of heating. Thanks to the possibility of obtaining data from individual sensors for a given period, it is possible to easily analyze the trend of changes in air quality for a given location. The platform, thanks to its intuitive interface, facilitates work with analyzing data from sensors.

Marcin Węgrzyn

Opole City, Head of the Department of Planning and Financing Environmental Protection City

I use the Airly Data Platform to monitor air quality measurements in the city of Toruń based on 50 Airly sensors. The platform is intuitive and easy to use. The graphics are friendly and visually nice. It allows you to obtain a wide range of statistical data from the beginning of the launch of the platform, which is useful for assessing the state of air pollution, an interesting feature is displaying the pollution forecast for the next day. In addition to the state of pollution, it shows meteorological data on an ongoing basis. The platform raises the interest of the city's inhabitants, which affects awareness of the state of air pollution and fulfills an educational role.

Mirosław Bulczyński

City of Toruń, Chief Specialist of the Department of Environment and Ecology

As a manager of the Łódź Regional Air Monitoring Network, I use the Airly Data Platform for monitoring air quality measurements from 90 Airly sensors. The Airly solution is intuitive and suitable for professionals who need to perform advanced analyzes of air pollution for selected locations and discover measurement trends and sources of pollution. The data elicited from the platform allows me easily share the results of analysis with marchal leaders and decide what actions should be taken for air pollution reduction.

Air Quality in the World

8% global rise in air pollution *

Between 2008-2013 air quality around the world deteriorated, particularly in poorer cities. The impact has not just been seen on the health of humans but animals and the environment too.

Airly's network of sensors builds up a picture over time so you can see the problems and monitor progress.

* Source: who.int

7 million premature deaths a year *

Air pollution is the fourth-leading risk factor for deaths worldwide. Higher than HIV, Malaria and Tuberculosis combined.

Airly's historic data tracks patterns and hotspots, empowering you to make changes to your local community.

* Source: theguardian.com

$Billions in lost revenue annually *

Billions are sacrificed in revenue each year due to lost labour income from sickness and death. Productivity levels amongst workers also increases 5-6% when air pollution levels are rated as 'good'.

Airly sensors detect local pollution levels and provide accurate information on air quality. This data can be used to implement changes, for a happier, healthier workforce.

* Source: worldbank.org

91% of people breathe polluted air *

Exposure to polluted air increases the risk of stroke, heart disease, lung cancer, and chronic and acute respiratory diseases, including asthma.

Airly is building a dense network of sensors around the globe to increase understanding and awareness of the problems we face.

* Source: who.int

Damaging every organ in the body *

Air quality can impact all areas of our health. From heart and lung disease to diabetes, dementia, cancers, brittle bones and damaged skin. Fertility, foetuses and children are also affected.

MyAirly's real time map view and pollution forecast, helps you to take care of yourself, friends and family members.

* Source: The Forum of International Respiratory Societies (FIRS)

Associated with 23% of child bronchitis cases *

Babies and children are particularly vulnerable to air pollution as their lungs are still growing and developing.

MyAirly equips you with real time data on air quality. This enables you to set alerts that signal when pollution levels are too high, so you can take action minimise the impact on your health.

* Source: worldbank.org
